## Child Tax Credit: who qualifies and how to claim it

**Foundation ID:** F-17 | **Category:** Individuals & Families | **Difficulty:** Beginner | **Tax Year:** 2025

**One-Sentence Answer:** The Child Tax Credit is worth up to $2,000 per qualifying child under 17, with $1,700 refundable — meaning families can receive cash back even if they owe no taxes, as long as each child has a valid SSN.

**Action Required:** Verify each child has a valid SSN (not ITIN) and passes all 7 qualifying tests. Claim on Schedule 8812 — tax software handles this automatically.

## YOUTUBE SCRIPT (5–6 min)

**Title:** Child Tax Credit 2025 — Who Qualifies and How to Claim It

**[HOOK]** One of the most valuable tax benefits for families in the U.S. is the Child Tax Credit — up to $2,000 per qualifying child. Yet many immigrant families who qualify never claim it.

**[THE BASICS]** For Tax Year 2025: $2,000 per qualifying child under 17. Up to $1,700 of that is refundable — meaning you can receive it as a cash refund even if you owe zero taxes. The phase-out begins at $200,000 AGI (single) or $400,000 (married filing jointly).

**[7 TESTS]** Your child must pass all seven qualifying tests: Age (under 17), Relationship (your child or close relative), Residency (lived with you more than half the year), Dependency (claimed as dependent), Support (child didn't support themselves), Joint Return (child didn't file jointly), and SSN (valid Social Security Number — not ITIN).

**[SSN REQUIREMENT]** This last test stops many immigrant families. The Child Tax Credit requires a valid SSN for each qualifying child. An ITIN is not sufficient. If your child has citizenship or work authorization, apply for an SSN for them. Children without SSN eligibility may qualify for the Credit for Other Dependents ($500, non-refundable).

**[REFUNDABLE PART]** Here's what most people miss: even if you owe no taxes, you may still receive up to $1,700 per child as a cash refund through the Additional Child Tax Credit. You must file a tax return to claim it. The money doesn't come automatically.

**[HOW TO CLAIM]** List qualifying children on Form 1040. Complete Schedule 8812 — tax software does this automatically when you enter your children's information. The software calculates both the non-refundable portion (reduces taxes) and the refundable ACTC (adds to refund).
