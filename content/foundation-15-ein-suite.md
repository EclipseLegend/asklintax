---

**[WHAT IS AN EIN — 0:25 to 1:00]**

EIN stands for Employer Identification Number. It's a nine-digit number the IRS assigns to identify your business for tax purposes. Think of it as a Social Security Number for your business.

Despite the name, you don't need employees to get one. Solo LLCs, freelancers, and single-person businesses all get EINs. The main reasons: to open a business bank account, avoid sharing your personal SSN with clients, and establish your business as a separate entity.

---

**[DO YOU NEED ONE — 1:00 to 1:45]**

You definitely need an EIN if: you formed an LLC or corporation, you want to hire employees, you have a solo 401(k) or Keogh plan, or you want to open a business bank account.

It's optional but recommended if you're a sole proprietor with no employees who doesn't mind using their SSN.

My general advice: get one anyway. It's free, takes 15 minutes, and there's no reason not to have one.

---

**[HOW TO APPLY — 1:45 to 4:00]**

Go to IRS.gov/ein. This is the official, free IRS application. Do not use any other website — many third-party sites charge $50 to $300 for a service that is completely free from the IRS.

Click Apply Online Now. The application is available Monday through Friday, 7am to 10pm Eastern time. You must complete it in one session — it can't be saved.

Step one: select your entity type. Sole proprietor, LLC, corporation, or partnership. If you have an LLC, choose Limited Liability Company — even if it's single-member.

Step two: answer questions about your business — state of operation, why you're applying, whether you've had an EIN before, and your business start date.

Step three: enter your personal information. Your name and Social Security Number or ITIN as the "responsible party." This is required for identity verification. The IRS does not use it to file anything on your behalf — it's just to verify who controls the entity.

Step four: review and submit. Double-check your business name spelling — it should match your LLC formation documents exactly.

Step five: receive your EIN immediately on screen. Download the confirmation page — called CP 575 — as a PDF right now. Save it somewhere safe. This is your official EIN letter, and banks will ask for it.

---

**[NO SSN — 4:00 to 4:45]**

If you don't have a Social Security Number — you're an international applicant or you only have an ITIN — you cannot use the online application.

Instead, call 267-941-1099. This is not toll-free. Call Monday through Friday, 6am to 11pm Eastern time. Have your ITIN or foreign tax ID ready. They will issue your EIN during the call.

---

**[AFTER YOUR EIN — 4:45 to 5:20]**

Once you have your EIN: open a business bank account. Bring your EIN, your LLC documents, and a government ID. Update your W-9 form to use your EIN instead of your personal SSN. Set up accounting software with your EIN so it appears on invoices automatically.

## AI KNOWLEDGE SNIPPET

**EIN (Employer Identification Number):**

9-digit number assigned by IRS to identify a business entity for tax purposes. Like an SSN for a business.

**Who needs one:** LLCs (all), corporations, partnerships, employers, Solo 401(k)/Keogh plan holders, anyone wanting to open a business bank account or avoid sharing personal SSN with clients.

**Who doesn't need one:** Sole proprietors with no employees, no retirement plans, willing to use SSN (optional but recommended to get one anyway).

**How to apply:**
- Online: IRS.gov/ein — free, instant, Mon–Fri 7am–10pm ET. Requires SSN or ITIN.
- Phone: 267-941-1099 — for international applicants without SSN/ITIN. Mon–Fri 6am–11pm ET. Instant.
- Fax: Form SS-4 → 4 business days
- Mail: Form SS-4 → 4–5 weeks

**After receiving EIN:**
1. Download and save CP 575 confirmation PDF immediately
2. Open business bank account (bring EIN + LLC docs + government ID)
3. Update W-9 to use EIN instead of SSN
4. Enter EIN in accounting software

**Warning:** Many third-party websites charge $50–$300 to "apply for an EIN." These are not affiliated with the IRS. The application is free at IRS.gov/ein.

**If lost:** Call IRS Business & Specialty Tax Line: 800-829-4933, Mon–Fri 7am–7pm local time.
