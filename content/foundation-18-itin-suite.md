## What is an ITIN and how do I apply?

**Foundation ID:** F-18 | **Category:** Individuals & Families | **Difficulty:** Beginner | **Tax Year:** 2025

**One-Sentence Answer:** An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a tax ID issued by the IRS for people not eligible for an SSN — apply using Form W-7 submitted with your tax return, and use a Certified Acceptance Agent to avoid mailing your original passport.

**Action Required:** If you need to file a U.S. tax return and don't have an SSN, apply for an ITIN via Form W-7. Use a CAA to verify documents. Allow 7–11 weeks. ITINs expire after 3 years of non-use — renew before filing if yours has lapsed.

## YOUTUBE SCRIPT (5–6 min)

**Title:** What Is an ITIN and How Do I Apply? (Complete Guide for Immigrants)

**[HOOK]** If you need to file a U.S. tax return but don't have a Social Security Number, you need an ITIN. Today I'll explain exactly what it is, who needs one, and how to apply without risking your passport.

**[WHAT IS ITIN]** ITIN stands for Individual Taxpayer Identification Number. It's a 9-digit number starting with 9, issued by the IRS — not the Social Security Administration. It's for tax purposes only. It does not authorize you to work, does not affect your immigration status, and is not shared with immigration authorities.

**[WHO NEEDS ONE]** You need an ITIN if you're not eligible for an SSN but have U.S. tax obligations. Most common: F-1 or J-1 students with non-wage U.S. income, resident aliens without SSN eligibility, dependent spouses or children of U.S. residents who don't have SSNs.

**[WHAT IT CANNOT DO]** An ITIN cannot: authorize work, qualify you for EITC or Child Tax Credit, provide Social Security benefits, or improve immigration status. These limitations are firm.

**[HOW TO APPLY]** Complete Form W-7. Attach a completed tax return (most applicants). Gather identity documents — a valid passport is the simplest because it proves both identity and foreign status by itself. Choose your submission method.

**[CAA RECOMMENDATION]** My strong recommendation: use a Certified Acceptance Agent. A CAA can verify your documents on-site and certify copies — so you never mail your original passport. Many accounting firms and university international student offices are CAAs. Find one at IRS.gov/itin.

**[PROCESSING]** Allow 7–11 weeks. You'll receive a CP565 letter with your ITIN.

**[EXPIRATION]** ITINs that haven't been used on a federal return for 3 consecutive years expire automatically. If yours has expired, renew with Form W-7 before filing.

## SHORTS SCRIPTS

**Short #1:** "Never Mail Your Passport — Use a CAA"
*Hook:* "Applying for an ITIN? Don't mail your original passport to the IRS."
The IRS accepts certified copies if they're verified by a Certified Acceptance Agent.
A CAA checks your documents in person and certifies copies for the IRS. Your originals never leave your hands.
Find a CAA at IRS.gov/itin. Many university international student offices offer this free.
Much safer than mailing a passport you might need.

**Short #2:** "ITIN Expires If Unused"
*Hook:* "Your ITIN might be expired and you don't know it."
ITINs that haven't appeared on a federal tax return for 3 consecutive years expire automatically.
If you haven't filed in a few years, your ITIN may no longer be valid.
Renew it before filing — use Form W-7, check "Renew an Existing ITIN."
Filing with an expired ITIN causes processing delays.

**Short #3:** "ITIN vs SSN — What's the Difference?"
*Hook:* "ITIN and SSN — both 9-digit numbers, very different things."
SSN: issued by Social Security Administration. Allows work. Doesn't expire.
ITIN: issued by IRS only. Tax purposes only. Expires if unused.
SSN starts with any digit. ITIN always starts with 9.
ITIN holders: can file returns and get refunds. Cannot claim EITC or Child Tax Credit.
